Most facilities teams spend their days reacting to problems. IoT-connected infrastructure flips that model - surfacing failures before they happen and cutting operational costs by 30–40%.

## The Cost of Reactive Maintenance

Every unplanned equipment failure costs more than a scheduled replacement. There's the direct repair cost, the disruption to building occupants, the emergency call-out premium, and the reputational impact of a facility that doesn't work properly. Multiply that across HVAC systems, lifts, lighting infrastructure, and utilities, and the cost of ignorance becomes significant.

![IoT sensor network in commercial building showing real-time equipment monitoring](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1518770660439-4636190af475?w=1200&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

## Sensor Layer: What to Monitor

A pragmatic IoT rollout starts with the highest-value monitoring points:

- **HVAC systems** - temperature, humidity, energy consumption, vibration in compressors and fans. Anomalies predict failures 2–4 weeks before they occur.
- **Electrical infrastructure** - power quality monitoring catches transformer degradation and circuit overloads before trips or fires.
- **Water systems** - flow sensors and pressure monitoring detect leaks at their earliest stages, before structural damage.
- **Space utilisation** - occupancy sensors inform cleaning schedules, desk allocation, and energy management.
- **Access and security** - door/window sensors, camera analytics, and badge integration create a complete operational picture.

## From Raw Data to Actionable Intelligence

Sensors produce data. Intelligence comes from the platform that processes it. The key components are:

**Edge computing** - sensors generate too much data to stream raw to a central server. Edge gateways pre-aggregate and filter, sending only meaningful signals upstream.

**Anomaly detection** - statistical models learn normal equipment behaviour and flag deviations. What looks like noise to a human might be the early signature of a bearing failure.

**Automated work order generation** - when an anomaly crosses a threshold, the platform creates a maintenance ticket, assigns the right technician, and schedules the work - without human intervention.

## The Business Case

A 100,000 sq ft commercial property typically spends £180–240K per year on reactive maintenance. IoT-driven predictive maintenance programmes consistently deliver 25–35% reductions in that spend, with an average payback period of 18–24 months.

The operational shift - from firefighting to planned, optimised maintenance - also reduces staff stress and improves tenant satisfaction scores.
