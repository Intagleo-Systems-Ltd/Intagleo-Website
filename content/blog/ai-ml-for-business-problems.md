## The AI Trap

Every founder wants AI in their product. ChatGPT is changing the world. Why shouldn't they?

Because most AI projects fail.

In 2023, 87% of ML projects never reached production. They were research projects that looked good in presentations but couldn't solve real business problems.

The issue: teams are asking "How do we use AI?" instead of "What problem are we solving?"

![Data science team reviewing model performance on large dashboards](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1460925895917-afdab827c52f?w=1200&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

## When AI Actually Works

We've built ML systems that generated millions in value. Here's what they had in common:

### 1. **Clear, Measurable Business Outcome**

Not "improve user experience." Specific: "Reduce support ticket volume by 40%" or "Increase conversion rate from 2% to 3%."

Why? Because you need to know if it works. And you need to know if it's worth the cost.

### 2. **High-Quality Training Data**

Garbage in, garbage out.

For a fraud detection system we built: 2 years of historical transactions, millions of data points, clear labels for fraud. Result: 98% accuracy, saved $12M/year.

### 3. **Humans in the Loop**

The best systems have ML making recommendations, humans making decisions.

A content moderation system that flags inappropriate posts for humans to review? Perfect. Automatic banning with no appeal? Disaster.

### 4. **Simple Models First**

We always start with boring stuff: logistic regression, random forests, linear regression.

If a $50/month model gets you 90% of the way there, why build a $5000/month neural network for the last 10%?

## Real Examples

**Customer churn prediction** (Subscription SaaS)
- Model: Random forest classifier
- Result: 78% accuracy. Led to $2M/year in retained revenue

**Demand forecasting** (E-commerce)
- Model: LSTM neural network
- Result: 85% forecast accuracy (vs. 60% with historical averages)
- ROI: $8M/year in recovered sales and reduced inventory costs

**Personalised recommendations** (Content platform)
- Model: Collaborative filtering + content-based hybrid
- Result: 23% increase in watch time, $15M/year in increased engagement

## The Framework We Use

**Stage 1: Discovery (2 weeks)** - Define the problem, validate data availability, quantify ROI.

**Stage 2: Prototype (4 weeks)** - Build a baseline model, test against the metric, decide if it's worth pursuing.

**Stage 3: Production (8–12 weeks)** - Build a production-worthy model with monitoring and retraining pipelines.

**Stage 4: Iteration (Ongoing)** - Monitor performance, retrain as data changes, A/B test against previous versions.
