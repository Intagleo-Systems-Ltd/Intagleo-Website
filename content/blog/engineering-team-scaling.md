Every CTO faces the capacity dilemma: too much work, not enough engineers. Permanent hires, contractors, or augmentation — the right answer depends on five factors.

## The Four Capacity Options

1. **Permanent hire** — highest commitment, highest cost, longest lead time (3–6 months), best for core roles
2. **Staff augmentation** — fast (days to weeks), flexible, best for defined scope with time boundary
3. **Project outsourcing** — hands off a defined deliverable to an external team; highest risk for core product work
4. **Tooling and automation** — removes capacity bottlenecks without headcount; often underweighted in the analysis

Most capacity decisions should start by asking whether the need can be addressed by tooling before adding people.

## The Five Decision Factors

**1. Duration** — needs lasting less than 12 months favour augmentation; permanent needs favour permanent hires. The crossover point depends on your cost model and talent availability.

**2. Specificity** — well-defined technical requirements (React expertise, AWS infrastructure, mobile iOS) are well-suited to augmentation. Vague or evolving requirements — "help us build the product" — need permanent hires who grow with the problem.

**3. Core vs. peripheral** — work that touches your core product, customer data, or competitive differentiation should be done by your permanent team, augmented if needed. Peripheral work (internal tools, one-time migrations) is safer to outsource.

**4. Knowledge risk** — if the work will generate knowledge that is critical to ongoing operations, ensure knowledge transfer is built into the engagement, not added as an afterthought.

**5. Culture fit requirements** — permanent team members need to embody company values and work style over years. Augmented staff need to integrate for months. Project outsourcing teams need to integrate minimally. The higher the culture fit requirement, the more permanent the engagement should be.

## Cost Modelling

The fully loaded cost of a permanent senior engineer (salary, benefits, employer taxes, equipment, office, recruiting) typically runs 1.5–1.8× base salary. Augmentation rates vary widely but typically cost 2–3× permanent salary equivalent on a daily rate basis, with no recruiting, notice period, or benefit overhead.

For engagements shorter than 9–12 months, augmentation is typically cheaper. For longer engagements, permanent hiring becomes competitive.
